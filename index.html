About the Workshop
Creativity. Connection.
Confidence.

This workshop invites girls to use art as a tool for self-expression, reflection, and confidence building. Over four weeks, students will experiment with ink, watercolor, acrylics, and oil pastels — guided by local artist Laura McDonald in a small, supportive studio environment.

No prior art experience required. This is about finding your creative voice, trusting your instincts, and making something you're proud of. Skills that last a lifetime.

🎨
Foundational Art Technique
Students work with multiple mediums — ink, watercolor, acrylics, and oil pastels — building real skills and creative decision-making confidence along the way.
💬
Meaningful Connection
A small group of ten means every girl gets real attention and real community. The kind of experience kids talk about years later.
🖼️
Finished Work to Keep
Every student leaves with completed pieces she made herself — artwork to hang, share, and be genuinely proud of.

Meet Laura
McDonald

Laura McDonald is a local Cincinnati artist who brings both technical skill and genuine warmth to everything she teaches. Working across ink, watercolor, acrylics, and oil pastels, she has a gift for meeting students exactly where they are.

She'll be joined by Girlhood Collective founder Brittany Gruber to create the kind of studio experience girls remember — where they feel seen, supported, and seriously proud of what they made.

Questions
Good to Know
Before You Register
Does my daughter need prior art experience?
Not at all. This workshop is designed for all levels. Laura meets students exactly where they are — the whole point is discovering what you're capable of, not showing up with skills you already have.
What does my daughter need to bring?
Nothing. All art supplies are included — ink, watercolor, acrylics, oil pastels, and everything needed for each session. Just show up ready to create.
What happens after I register and pay?
We'll reach out within a day or two with registration paperwork and any details you need before the first session on April 8th. Payment reserves your spot.
Is this a drop-off program?
Yes. Sessions run 4:00–5:30 PM at The Columbia Center, 3500 Columbia Parkway. Parents are welcome to wait or return at 5:30 PM for pickup.
What if we need to miss a session?
Life happens — reach out to us at hello@cincinnatigirl.com and we'll do our best to make it work. Because the group is small, consistent attendance makes the experience better for everyone.
Will there be more programs after this one?
